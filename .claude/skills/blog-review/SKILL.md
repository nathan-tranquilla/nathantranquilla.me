# Blog Draft Review

TRIGGER WHEN: reviewing, evaluating, or editing a blog post draft in `src/pages/blogs/`

## Voice and Style

- Preserve the author's voice. Only fix what is asked. Do not rewrite sentences to sound "better" unless asked.
- Never use em dashes. Use colons, semicolons, periods, or restructure the sentence instead.
- Target an 8th to 10th grade reading level. Use short, clear sentences. Avoid jargon unless the audience expects it. Prefer common words over fancy ones.
- Do not add emojis.

## Review Process

1. **Critique first, edit second.** When asked to evaluate, give feedback without making changes. Wait for approval before editing.
2. **Fix only what is requested.** If asked to fix typos, fix typos. Do not also restructure paragraphs, add transitions, or "improve" wording.
3. **Flag issues by category:**
   - Typos and grammar
   - Sentence structure (awkward phrasing, run-ons, fragments)
   - Redundancy (same idea said twice)
   - Weak hooks or buried leads
   - Reading level (overly complex language)
   - Dangling references (e.g. "that" or "this" pointing to something removed)
4. **Section-by-section.** Review one section at a time when the post is long. Do not dump all feedback at once.
5. **Second opinion on completion.** When the author considers the draft done (or close to done), use a subagent to get a fresh critique before finalizing. The subagent should read the full post and give honest feedback on structure, hooks, clarity, and whether the post commits to a clear angle. Surface the subagent's feedback to the author for their consideration.

## Writing Conventions

- Capitalize product names correctly (Claude, Claude Code, Claude skills).
- Use colons to introduce lists or elaborations, not em dashes.
- Keep paragraphs short. Three to five sentences max.
- Numbered lists should use consistent parallel structure.
- Code references use backticks (e.g. `.claude/skills/`).

## Commit Workflow

- Commit when a section is approved by the author.
- Do not batch multiple sections into one commit unless asked.
