A buyer's guide for photographers hiring a web developer. Know what you're paying for, what it should cost, and how to tell if a quote is fair.

### What's inside

1. **Template vs. Custom** — the biggest cost driver, explained with pros, cons, and price ranges for each
2. **What You're Paying For** — galleries, design implementation, CMS, e-commerce, SEO, hosting, and maintenance broken down by complexity
3. **What Should It All Cost?** — three pricing tiers from $500 to $20,000+ so you know where you fit
4. **Services Worth Using Instead of Building** — third-party tools that are cheaper and better than custom work
5. **Evaluating a Quote** — what a good quote includes, questions to ask, red flags, and green flags
6. **The Process** — a step-by-step approach to hiring, from requirements to launch
7. **Glossary** — plain-English definitions for CMS, CDN, SEO, and every other term developers throw around

### Who this is for

You're ready to hire a web developer but you don't know what's reasonable. You've seen quotes ranging from $500 to $15,000 and can't tell why. This guide gives you the language and the framework to evaluate any quote, ask the right questions, and avoid overpaying for things you don't need. Written with photographers in mind, but applicable to anyone commissioning a portfolio or small business site.
